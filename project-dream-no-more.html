Dream no more

3D Game · Walking simulator · Dreamcore

Overview

A first-person wandering game through liminal rooms, pool-blue corridors and empty stairwells where time feels stalled. Fragments of text and sound gradually form a memory about letting go.

Notes

Spaces are built to feel “purposeful yet purposeless”: signage without direction, doors without rooms. Palette oscillates between cyan and lilac to echo dreamcore screenshots. There is no fail state—only the quiet end of a corridor.

Dream No More — Meta Walking Simulator (UE5 / Unity HDRP)

Context

RMIT research project exploring a slow-burn, non-jumpscare approach to horror through dreamcore/poolcore aesthetics. Goal: tell story through space, light, and micro-interactions rather than dialogue.

Action

  • Built three playable prototypes in UE5: a white “swing room,” a looping pool corridor, and a suburban forced-perspective house. Implemented Blueprint level logic and portal progression.
  • Designed core interactions: hand-push swing and proximity-revealed door prompts (F) leading to seamless transitions; kept keyboard fallbacks and left hooks for MediaPipe gestures and TouchDesigner audio reactivity.
  • Established a unified look pipeline using HDRP/Post (SSGI, bloom, fog) and documented reusable templates for gates, loops, and atmosphere timing.
  • Ran weekly iteration cycles with mentor feedback; maintained naming conventions and a small art/tech bible for repeatability.

Result

  • Delivered a stable first-person prototype set achieving the intended “quiet pressure” mood.
  • Shipped a reusable technical/art baseline (gesture/audio interfaces, portal prefab, loop pattern) that accelerates future levels and showcases well in coursework demos.
